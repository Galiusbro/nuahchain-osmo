## 3. Gaps & What Would Likely Need to Be Built / Adapted
From comparing the full list of functionalities vs what exists, here are some of the gaps / areas that would need work:
Full options markets (calls, puts, exotic options) with good liquidity / user experience, settlement, pricing, exercise, etc. The “simple‑option” sample contracts exist, but production grade options markets are less mature in Cosmos.
Credit / unsecured lending: most DeFi still uses over‑collateralization. If one tried to replicate unsecured or under‑collateralized lending, need risk models, credit scoring, maybe oracles / reputation.
Securities tokenization + compliance layers: for dividend/coupon payments, regulatory compliance, identity / KYC layers.
Clearinghouse / default fund / systemic risk protections (e.g. for derivatives) are less mature.
Permanent insurance / reinsurance pools built on Cosmos (though Nexus expanding) may not have fully open‑source Cosmos native versions.
Cross‑chain derivatives / cross‑asset risk hedging that goes beyond simple token swaps or perps.

## Gaps / Less-Well‑Covered Areas
These are functionalities you likely will need to build or adapt more heavily; I did **not** find strong examples of open‑source, production‑audited Cosmos modules / contracts for them (at least as of current public information):
**General options (calls, puts) with flexible strikes, American/European styles, volatility modeling**: beyond binary options, I did *not* find a fully audited general purpose options protocol in Cosmos‑native open source that supports more complex option types.
**Exotic derivatives / structured products** (e.g. barrier options, lookback options, etc.) are not well covered.
**Under‑collateralized / unsecured lending / credit scoring systems**: Cosmos DeFi tends toward over‑collateralization; unsecured credit is rare/publicly available and audited.
**Reinsurance / parametric insurance / insurance with external triggers beyond the typical “hack / protocol failure”**: less evidence of mature modules.
**Clearinghouse / default fund designs for derivatives beyond what Injective offers**: particularly for large leveraged exposure, cross‐market default risk, etc.
**Compliance / identity / tokenized securities** (i.e. modular KYC / securities regulation) — almost no audited modules in the open that I found.
**Margin trading / leverage trading beyond what’s built into some derivatives platforms** (depending on the exact design) — in general, needs careful risk modeling and audit.
